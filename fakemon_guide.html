What Is a Fakemon?

A Fakemon is a made-up battle creature that you design! It lives inside a JSON file — a special text file the game reads when it starts. You decide its name, what it looks like, how strong it is, and what moves it can use.

💡 The game figures out your Fakemon's power level on its own by running 200 pretend battles in the background. So the game will always find you a fair opponent — no matter how strong your Fakemon is!
2

The 5 Stats

Every Fakemon has five stats. These numbers describe how good your Fakemon is at different things. Some moves can raise or lower these stats mid-battle!

❤️ Health
field: "hp"
How much damage your Fakemon can take before fainting. When this hits zero, you lose!
Suggested: 80–140
⚔️ Power
field: "power"
A multiplier on all your damage numbers. Higher power = every attack hits harder.
Suggested: 80–120
💨 Speed
field: "speed"
When two moves have the same speed tier (like both are "medium"), the Fakemon with higher speed goes first.
Suggested: 60–120
🛡️ Defense
field: "defense"
Reduces incoming damage by a percentage. A defense of 20 means you only take 80% of the damage you'd normally receive.
Suggested: 0–40
🎯 Accuracy
field: "accuracy"
The base chance (%) that your moves will actually hit the target. Status effects like confuse or paralyze can reduce this!
Suggested: 70–100
💡 StatUp and StatDwn moves raise or lower these stats temporarily during battle. For example, a "Power Up" move might raise your Power stat by 20% for 3 turns — making all your damage moves hit harder while it lasts!

Status Effects

Status effects are conditions your moves can put on the enemy (or yourself!). They can turn the tide of a battle even when your damage isn't the highest.

Primary Statuses — Only one at a time!

A Fakemon can only have ONE primary status at a time. You can't poison AND burn the same enemy.

🟣
Poison
PRIMARY
The enemy loses 8% of their max HP at the end of every turn. It adds up fast — a Fakemon with 100 HP loses 8 HP each turn!
⏱ Lasts until healed or battle ends
🔴
Burn
PRIMARY
The enemy loses 6% of their max HP per turn AND their Power stat is reduced — so their attacks hit softer too. Double trouble!
⏱ Lasts until healed or battle ends
⚡
Paralyze
PRIMARY
Each turn, the enemy has a 35% chance of not being able to move at all. Their Speed stat also drops — so even if they do move, they might go second.
⏱ Lasts until healed or battle ends
💤
Sleep
PRIMARY
The enemy can't do anything for 2–3 turns. They just snooze while you attack freely! They wake up on their own when the turns run out.
⏱ 2–3 turns, then auto-wakes

Secondary Statuses — Can stack with primary ones!

😵
Confuse
SECONDARY
The confused enemy has a 33% chance of accidentally hurting themselves instead of attacking you. They might punch themselves in the face!
⏱ 2–4 turns, then wears off
😦
Flinch
SECONDARY
The enemy skips their move this turn because they flinched from being hit. Only works if your move hits first — fast moves flinch better!
⏱ Only the current turn
📈
Stat Up
SECONDARY
Temporarily raises one of YOUR stats — like Power, Speed, or Defense. Decide in the move which stat goes up and by how much. Fades after a few turns.
⏱ 3 turns
📉
Stat Down
SECONDARY
Temporarily lowers one of the ENEMY's stats — like Power, Speed, or Accuracy. Makes them weaker or slower while the effect lasts.
⏱ 3 turns
⚠️ Important: Stacking the same status doesn't help! If your Fakemon has five sleep moves, the game treats them all as one — it only gives you credit for one sleep move. Variety is way more powerful than repetition!

Designing Your Moves

Your Fakemon can have up to 8 moves. Each move is an object inside the "moves" list of your JSON file.

Field Required? What to put here
id ✅ Yes A short nickname with no spaces — like fire_bite or thunder_kick. Every move must have a different id.
name ✅ Yes The name players see in battle, including an emoji! Example: "🔥 Ember Bite"
desc ✅ Yes A short 1–2 sentence description of what the move does.
speed ✅ Yes fast goes first and hits 15% harder  |  medium is normal  |  slow goes last but can be extra powerful
damage ✅ Yes Two numbers: [min, max]. The attack does a random amount between these. Use [0, 0] for no damage, or [-25, -25] to heal yourself 25 HP.
status Optional The status effect to apply. Choose from: poison, burn, paralyze, sleep, confuse, flinch, statup, statdwn. Use null for no effect.
statusChance Optional How likely the status is to apply. 1.0 = always works. 0.5 = 50% chance. 0.35 = 35% chance. Use 0 if no status.
💡 Strategy tip: A Fakemon with ALL damage moves has no tricks. A Fakemon with ALL status moves but no damage might never finish the fight. Mix it up — maybe 3–4 damage moves, 2 status moves, 1 stat move, and 1 heal move is a good balance!

Naming Your Fakemon

StrategyHow it worksExample
Mash two words Take an animal and an element, smoosh them together Bear + Blizzard = Blizzear
Sound matches vibe K, X, Z sounds feel fierce. L, M, W sounds feel mysterious. Voltusk vs Glopple
Name what it does Think about its main attack or ability Burns things → Embork, Scorchwing
Just make it weird Silly and fun is totally valid! Blobzap, Quakefuzz, Snorblast

Adding Your Fakemon to the Game

Two steps and you're in!

StepWhat to do
1️⃣ Put your file (e.g. myfakemon.json) inside the fakemon/ folder.
2️⃣ Open fakemon/fakemonlist.json and add your Fakemon's id to the list.

Here's what fakemonlist.json looks like — just add your id at the end:

{
  "fakemon": [
    "embork",
    "glopple",
    "voltusk",
    "yourfakemon"   // ← add yours here!
  ]
}
🎮 Reload the game in your browser and your Fakemon will show up on the selection screen with its power level automatically calculated. Time to battle!

Strategy Tips

The power system is smart — it actually simulates battles to score your Fakemon. Here's how to make something genuinely good:

✅ Do this

  • Mix damage moves with status moves
  • Use different status types — variety counts!
  • Put strong attacks on fast — they go first AND hit harder
  • Add a heal move if you have high HP
  • Use slow moves for your big power moves
  • Think about your type matchup — Fire destroys Nature!

❌ Don't bother with

  • Five of the same sleep move — only counts once
  • All damage, no status — too predictable
  • All status, no damage — you'll never finish the fight
  • Crazy high HP hoping to outlast — the matchmaker adapts
  • Ignoring defense — even a little helps a lot
  • 100% accuracy on everything — spread it around
🏆 The best Fakemon aren't necessarily the strongest — they're the most interesting to play! A clever design with good synergy between moves will beat a boring high-damage machine most of the time.

Final Checklist

Before you turn in your Fakemon, check every box:

  • My id has no spaces or special characters
  • My filename matches my id exactly — e.g. blazkit.json for id "blazkit"
  • I added my filename to fakemonlist.json
  • My Fakemon has between 4 and 8 moves
  • I have at least one move that deals damage
  • All 5 stats are filled in (hp, power, speed, defense, accuracy)
  • I filled in createdBy with my full name
  • I picked an emoji for my Fakemon
  • I tested it in the game and it loads without errors
  • My Fakemon has a description that shows its personality
🐉 Good luck, and have fun! The weirdest, most creative Fakemon are usually the most fun to battle with. Make it yours!
